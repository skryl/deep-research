## OpenAI

### "OpenAI Is the WeWork of AI" (Late 2023 - Ongoing)

**Source**: [Marcus on AI Substack](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/is-openai-more-like-wework-or-theranos), [MSN](https://www.msn.com/en-us/movies/news/gary-marcus-openai-could-be-the-next-wework/vi-AA1u6F3U)

**Prediction**: Unless GPT-5 is spectacular, OpenAI will eventually be viewed as the WeWork of AI -- greatly overvalued relative to what it could deliver. Charismatic founder, immense valuation, questionable business plan, potential for massive deflation.

Marcus specifically compared: "charismatic founder, immense valuation, questionable business plan, and the possibility of similar immense deflation." He leaned WeWork over Theranos since OpenAI ships real products, even if they underperform expectations.

**Assessment (March 2026)**: PARTIALLY CORRECT on substance, wrong on timing. OpenAI has struggled with profitability, leadership turmoil (the Altman firing/rehiring in November 2023), and GPT-5 underwhelmed. However, OpenAI secured a $150B+ valuation round, showing investor confidence has not collapsed. Marcus himself admitted he was "flat out wrong about investors."

### Three Reasons OpenAI Struggles to Profit

**Prediction**: (1) The product is unreliable, (2) it is expensive to operate, and (3) they have no technical moat -- leading to price wars.

**Assessment (March 2026)**: LARGELY CORRECT. All three factors have played out. Competitors (Anthropic, Google, Meta, DeepSeek) have caught up or surpassed OpenAI on various benchmarks. Price competition has been fierce. OpenAI's operating costs remain enormous.

### GPT-5 Delays

**Source**: [Twitter/X](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/1788269696690642990)

**Prediction** (May 2024): "Nothing in 2024 will be worthy of the name GPT-5" and "much of the year will be spent hyping a speculative future product while releasing only 4.X versions."

**Assessment**: CORRECT. GPT-5 was not released in 2024. OpenAI released GPT-4o, GPT-4o mini, and o1/o3 reasoning models, but nothing branded as GPT-5 until 2025.

### Competitors Will Narrow the Gap

**Prediction**: "Competitors will continue to narrow the gap" and "OpenAI will never again be as dominant as it was in 2023."

**Assessment (March 2026)**: CORRECT. Claude (Anthropic), Gemini (Google), Llama (Meta), and DeepSeek have all significantly closed or eliminated the gap with OpenAI's models on major benchmarks.

### Masayoshi Son / SoftBank Connection

**Source**: [Marcus on AI Substack](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/does-openais-new-financing-make-sense)

Marcus noted that one of the biggest investors in OpenAI was Masayoshi Son -- who was also the biggest investor in WeWork before its collapse. He cited this as a warning sign.

### Copyright Liability

**Source**: [Twitter/X](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/1740929704167924163)

**Prediction**: "If OpenAI does collapse, WeWork style, it will likely be seen as a tale of hubris. They knew infringement was going to be a major issue. They proceeded anyway. That will not play well in front of juries."

**Assessment (March 2026)**: PENDING. Copyright lawsuits against OpenAI (NYT v. OpenAI and others) remain active. No final verdicts yet, but the legal exposure is real and significant.

## Sora (OpenAI Video)

**Source**: [Marcus on AI Substack](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/sora-cant-handle-the-truth), [Sora's Surreal Physics](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/soras-surreal-physics)

**Prediction** (February-May 2024): Sora has not "learned physics," will be costly to run, and too unreliable for many purposes. The physics glitches are systemic, not data problems -- artifacts of "lossy compression" akin to LLM hallucinations.

Specific example: A Sora video of a monkey playing chess showed 3 kings, only one on the board, on a 7x7 board -- "utterly impossible in chess."

**Assessment (March 2026)**: CORRECT. The [systematic claims analysis](https://github.com/davegoldblatt/marcus-claims-dataset) scored Sora predictions at 90% supported, 0% contradicted. Sora launched in December 2024 to underwhelming reviews, with quality, cost, and reliability concerns widely reported.

## General Industry Predictions

### AI Agents Will Be Unreliable (2025)

**Source**: [25 AI Predictions for 2025](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/25-ai-predictions-for-2025-from-marcus)

**Prediction**: "AI Agents will be endlessly hyped throughout 2025 but far from reliable, except possibly in very narrow use cases."

**Assessment (March 2026)**: LARGELY CORRECT. The systematic analysis scored "agents premature for production" at 88% supported, 0% contradicted. Agents received enormous hype but enterprise deployment remained limited.

### Humanoid Robots Will Disappoint

**Source**: [25 AI Predictions for 2025](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/25-ai-predictions-for-2025-from-marcus), [Six Predictions for 2026](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/six-or-seven-predictions-for-ai-2026)

**Prediction**: "Humanoid robotics will see a lot of hype, but nobody will release anything remotely as capable as Rosie the Robot." Optimus and Figure will be "all demo and very little product."

**Assessment (March 2026)**: CORRECT. Humanoid robots remained in the demo/prototype stage with no consumer or broadly deployed commercial products.

### Amazon Alexa Won't Improve (November 2022)

**Prediction**: LLMs would not immediately markedly improve commercial AI agents like Amazon Alexa.

**Assessment (March 2026)**: CORRECT. Despite Amazon's investment in Alexa+ with LLM capabilities, voice assistants have not seen the transformative improvement many predicted.
