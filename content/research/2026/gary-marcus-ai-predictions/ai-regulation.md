## Senate Testimony (May 16, 2023)

**Source**: [Senate Judiciary Committee](https://www.judiciary.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/2023-05-16%20-%20Testimony%20-%20Marcus.pdf), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2023/05/18/psst-gary-marcus-is-happy-to-help-regulate-ai-on-behalf-of-the-u-s-government/)

Marcus testified alongside Sam Altman (OpenAI CEO) and Christina Montgomery (IBM) in the first major Senate hearing on AI regulation. Key proposals and predictions:

### Proposals

1. **FDA-like agency for AI**: Marcus proposed a regulatory body that evaluates large-scale AI deployment, balances risks, ensures compliance on bias and misinformation, and empowers independent evaluation. Both Altman and Marcus agreed on this point.
2. **Nutritional labels for AI**: Transparency requirements analogous to food labeling.
3. **International governance**: Regulation at both federal and international levels.

### Predictions and Warnings

| Prediction | Assessment (March 2026) |
|-----------|------------------------|
| "We acted too slowly with social media" and risk repeating the mistake with AI | CORRECT in spirit -- no comprehensive federal AI regulation passed |
| Big tech's preferred plan "boils down to 'trust us'" and shouldn't be accepted | CORRECT -- voluntary commitments from AI companies proved non-binding and inconsistently followed |
| AI's future is "destabilizing" | ON TRACK -- deepfakes, misinformation, and job displacement concerns have grown |
| Bipartisan cooperation on AI regulation was possible | PARTIALLY WRONG -- initial bipartisan interest faded, and by 2025-2026 AI regulation became politically polarized |

## Prediction: Regulation Won't Keep Pace

**Source**: [IVN](https://ivn.us/posts/gary-marcus-ai-regulation-us-political-system-wasnt-set-push-through-things-effectively-2024), [Marcus on AI Substack](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/jumpstarting-ai-governance)

Marcus warned that the US political system "wasn't set up to push through things effectively" and that the window for AI regulation could "slam shut for at least the next decade."

**Assessment (March 2026)**: CORRECT. Despite the 2023 hearing's momentum, no comprehensive federal AI legislation was passed. The EU's AI Act moved forward, but US regulation remained fragmented and largely voluntary. Under the Trump administration (from January 2025), the regulatory environment shifted toward deregulation.

---

## Regulatory Capture Warning

Marcus expressed concern about "regulatory capture" -- that shrewd executives at major tech companies might induce governments to adopt regulation that keeps out smaller players and entrenches Big Tech while doing little to address underlying risks.

**Assessment (March 2026)**: PRESCIENT. Debates around the EU AI Act and proposed US regulations frequently centered on compliance costs that disproportionately burden smaller companies, with big tech companies publicly supporting regulations that their scale enables them to meet.

## Copyright Lawsuits Prediction

**Source**: [25 AI Predictions for 2025](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/25-ai-predictions-for-2025-from-marcus)

**Prediction**: "Copyright lawsuits over generative AI will continue throughout the year."

**Assessment**: CORRECT. NYT v. OpenAI, Getty v. Stability AI, and numerous other copyright cases continued through 2025 and into 2026 with no definitive resolution.

## Misinformation and Democracy Warnings

During his Senate testimony, Marcus demonstrated GPT-4's ability to generate convincing fake news by presenting a fabricated story about aliens conspiring with the Senate -- noting that "there is no Angela Stone at Yale" and GPT-4 wrote it at his behest.

He has consistently warned that the most troubling AI risk is the threat to democracy from automatically generated misinformation and deepfakes. While AI-generated misinformation has become widespread (confirming his warnings), the effects on actual elections have been less catastrophic than his worst-case scenarios suggested.
