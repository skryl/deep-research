## Overview

Gary Marcus is known for putting his money where his mouth is, repeatedly challenging AI optimists to formal bets. His wagers have become a signature part of his public persona.

---

## The $100,000 Bet Challenge to Elon Musk (2022)

**Source**: [Artificial Intelligence News](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/gary-marcus-criticises-elon-musk-agi-prediction/)

**Date**: 2022

**Details**: Marcus challenged Musk to a $100,000 bet that AGI would not arrive by 2029, responding to Musk's prediction that AGI was imminent. The proposed stakes later rose to $500,000. AI researcher Melanie Mitchell (Santa Fe Institute) suggested the bets be placed on longbets.org, where the loser donates to charity.

**Outcome**: Musk never responded. The bet was never formalized.

---

## The $1 Million Challenge to Elon Musk (April 2024)

**Source**: [Twitter/X](https://x.com/garymarcus/status/1777730295586001097)

**Date**: April 2024

**Details**: After Musk predicted AI would be "smarter than any individual human" by end of 2025, Marcus offered a $1 million bet that this would not happen. Investor Damion Hankejh (CEO of ingk.com) escalated to a $10 million bet.

**Outcome**: Musk never responded. As of March 2026, Marcus appears vindicated -- no AI system achieved human-superior general intelligence by end of 2025.

## The Marcus-Brundage Bet (December 30, 2024)

**Source**: [Marcus on AI Substack](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/where-will-ai-be-at-the-end-of-2027), [Twitter/X](https://x.com/GaryMarcus/status/1873766399618785646)

**Date**: December 30, 2024

**Parties**: Gary Marcus vs. Miles Brundage (independent AI policy researcher, formerly Senior Advisor for AGI Readiness at OpenAI)

**Terms**: 10:1 odds. If AI can perform 8 of 10 specified tasks by end of 2027, Marcus donates $2,000 to Brundage's chosen charity. If fewer than 8, Brundage donates $20,000 to Marcus's chosen charity. Both pledged to donate regardless of outcome.

### The 10 Tasks

**Ordinary adult-level tasks (4):**

1. **Watch a movie** -- Watch a previously unseen mainstream movie and follow plot twists, know when to laugh, summarize without spoilers or fabrications, answer questions about characters, conflicts, motivations, and plot twists
2. **Read novels** -- Read new mainstream novels and reliably answer questions about plot, character, conflicts, motivations, going beyond the literal text
3. **Write biographies/obituaries** -- Write engaging brief biographies and obituaries without obvious hallucinations, grounded in reliable sources
4. **Master video games** -- Learn and master basics of almost any new video game within minutes/hours and solve original puzzles in the game world

**Expert-level tasks (2):**

5. **Write legal briefs** -- Write cogent, persuasive legal briefs without hallucinating any cases
6. **Write bug-free code** -- Reliably construct bug-free code of more than 10,000 lines from natural language specification (gluing together libraries doesn't count)

**Frontier-level tasks (4):**

7. **Write Pulitzer-caliber books** -- With little or no human involvement, write Pulitzer-caliber fiction and non-fiction
8. **Write Oscar-caliber screenplays** -- With little or no human involvement
9. **Nobel-caliber science** -- With little or no human involvement, paradigm-shifting discoveries
10. **Formalize mathematical proofs** -- Convert arbitrary natural-language proofs from mathematical literature into symbolic form for verification

### Current Status (March 2026)

- [Manifold prediction market](https://manifold.markets/dreev/will-miles-brundage-win-his-bet-wit): ~21% chance Brundage wins
- [Metaculus](https://www.metaculus.com/questions/31246/): Tracking community predictions
- Individual task estimates on Manifold range from ~83% (Task 2: novels) to ~52% (Task 4: video games)
- Marcus's 2025 prediction that "no single system will solve more than 4" of these tasks appeared on track

## The 10:1 Bet on 2027 AI Progress

**Source**: [Marcus on AI Substack](https://garymarcus.substack.com/p/where-will-ai-be-at-the-end-of-2027)

Marcus framed the 10:1 odds as reflecting his confidence: he's "quite sure" the majority of these tasks won't be solved by 2027. He argues that tasks requiring genuine understanding (movies, novels), reliability (legal briefs, code), and creativity (Pulitzer books, Nobel science) all expose fundamental LLM limitations.

## Unfulfilled Bet Offers

Marcus has also offered bets that went unaccepted:
- **$100K to Elon Musk** on AGI by 2029 (2022) -- no response
- **$500K to Elon Musk** (escalated) -- no response
- **$1M to Elon Musk** on human-superior AI by end 2025 (April 2024) -- no response
- **$10M** (offered by Damion Hankejh alongside Marcus) -- no response

The pattern of Musk declining to formalize any wager has itself become part of Marcus's rhetorical toolkit, suggesting that even Musk may not fully believe his own timelines.
